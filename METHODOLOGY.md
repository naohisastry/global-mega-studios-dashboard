# Methodology

This document explains the data definitions, assumptions, estimation methods, and limitations used in the Global Mega-Studios / Super-Indies Financial Dashboard.

本ファイルは、Global Mega-Studios / Super-Indies Financial Dashboard におけるデータ定義、推計方法、前提条件、制約事項を整理するための方法論ドキュメントです。

---

## 1. Purpose / 目的

This dashboard visualizes the financial scale, profitability, business model mix, and M&A-driven growth of major global and European production studio groups from 2015 to 2025.

本ダッシュボードは、2015年から2025年における欧米主要映像制作スタジオグループの売上規模、利益率、事業モデル構成、M&Aによる成長要因を可視化することを目的としています。

Covered companies:

- Banijay
- Fremantle
- ITV Studios
- BBC Studios
- Mediawan
- All3Media

---

## 2. Main Analytical Layers / 主な分析レイヤー

The dashboard is organized around four analytical layers.

1. Revenue trends
2. EBITA / EBITDA margin comparison
3. Business model mix
4. M&A timeline and ownership changes

本ダッシュボードでは、各社を単なる番組制作会社としてではなく、IP、フォーマット、流通、ライセンス、M&A統合を含むグローバルなコンテンツ事業体として比較します。

---

## 3. Data Categories / データ分類

The dashboard uses the following types of data.

### 3.1 Officially disclosed figures / 公式開示データ

Figures disclosed by companies, parent companies, investor relations materials, annual reports, financial statements, press releases, or official announcements.

Examples:

- Revenue
- EBITA
- EBITDA
- Adjusted EBITDA
- Operating profit
- Segment profit
- Ownership structure
- Acquisition announcements

### 3.2 Parent-company segment disclosures / 親会社のセグメント開示

Some companies are not listed independently and are consolidated within larger groups.

Examples:

- Fremantle within RTL Group / Bertelsmann
- ITV Studios within ITV plc
- BBC Studios within BBC Group / BBC Commercial
- Banijay within FL Entertainment / Banijay Group
- Mediawan through available corporate materials
- All3Media through ownership and transaction disclosures

### 3.3 Market research and industry estimates / 調査会社・業界推計

When official disclosure is unavailable or incomplete, the dashboard may use third-party reports, trade publications, financial media, and analyst estimates.

### 3.4 Analyst-derived assumptions / 補完推計

Some data points may be estimated by combining multiple public sources.

Examples:

- Business model mix where official segment data is not available
- Approximate revenue allocation by production, distribution, formats, and licensing
- M&A-driven growth contribution
- Currency-adjusted comparison
- Interpolation where annual figures are missing

---

## 4. Key Data Definitions / 主要データ定義

### 4.1 Revenue / 売上

Revenue generally refers to annual sales or turnover generated by the studio group or relevant production segment.

However, the scope may differ by company.

Revenue may include:

- Production revenue
- Distribution revenue
- Format sales
- Licensing revenue
- Streaming and digital revenue
- International sales
- Other commercial activities

Revenue should be interpreted as a scale indicator, not as a perfectly standardized accounting measure.

### 4.2 EBITA / EBITDA Margin / 利益率

Profitability metrics differ across companies.

Commonly used metrics include:

- EBITA
- EBITDA
- Adjusted EBITDA
- Operating profit
- Segment profit

The dashboard uses the most relevant and publicly available profitability metric for each company. These metrics are not always directly comparable.

When comparing margins, users should consider:

- Whether amortization is excluded
- Whether exceptional items are excluded
- Whether the figure is adjusted or unadjusted
- Whether the figure is segment-level or consolidated
- Whether the company uses calendar year or fiscal year reporting

### 4.3 Business Model Mix / 事業モデル構成

Business model mix represents the estimated composition of each studio group’s revenue and strategic activities.

Categories may include:

- Production
- Distribution
- Formats
- Licensing
- Streaming / digital
- Other commercial activities

When official segment breakdowns are unavailable, business model mix may be estimated from company disclosures, strategy materials, management commentary, M&A activity, and industry analysis.

### 4.4 M&A Timeline / M&Aタイムライン

The M&A timeline includes major acquisitions, mergers, ownership changes, and strategic transactions that affected company scale, portfolio, or business model.

Examples:

- Production company acquisitions
- Format library acquisitions
- Cross-border expansion deals
- Private equity ownership changes
- Consolidation of independent studios
- Corporate restructuring

---

## 5. Currency Treatment / 通貨の扱い

Companies may report in different currencies, including EUR and GBP.

For cross-company comparison, the dashboard may use currency conversion.

Important notes:

- Reported currency figures should be preferred where available.
- Converted figures are for analytical comparison only.
- Exchange rates may affect apparent growth trends.
- Currency conversion does not remove accounting differences.
- Some dashboard views may use EUR as a common comparison currency.

通貨換算は企業間比較のための補助的な処理であり、各社の公式報告数値そのものとは異なる場合があります。

---

## 6. Treatment of Estimates / 推計値の扱い

The dashboard includes both disclosed figures and estimates.

When a number is not directly disclosed, it should be treated as an analytical estimate rather than a confirmed fact.

Estimate reliability depends on:

- Availability of official source material
- Consistency across multiple sources
- Recency of the data
- Transparency of the original metric definition
- Whether the company is public, private, or owned by a larger group
- Whether the figure is affected by M&A or restructuring

本ダッシュボードでは、推計値を確定的な事実としてではなく、公開情報に基づく分析上の補完値として扱います。

---

## 7. Source Hierarchy / 出典の優先順位

When multiple sources exist, the following hierarchy is generally used.

1. Official company disclosures
2. Parent-company annual reports and investor presentations
3. Regulatory filings
4. Official press releases
5. Transaction announcements
6. Reputable financial media and trade publications
7. Market research and industry reports
8. Analyst-derived assumptions

公式発表、決算資料、IR資料、親会社のセグメント開示、M&A発表を優先し、それらで不足する部分については業界メディア、調査会社、補完推計を使用します。

## 8. Limitations / 制約事項

This dashboard has several limitations.

### 8.1 Different reporting structures / 開示構造の違い

The covered companies do not all disclose financial data in the same format.

Some are public companies, some are subsidiaries, and some are privately held or owned by investment groups.

As a result, data availability and comparability differ by company.

### 8.2 Different profit metrics / 利益指標の違い

EBITA, EBITDA, adjusted EBITDA, operating profit, and segment profit are not identical.

The dashboard uses the closest available profitability metric but does not imply full accounting equivalence.

### 8.3 M&A discontinuities / M&Aによる時系列の断絶

M&A can create sudden jumps in revenue, margin, or business model composition.

Time-series growth should be interpreted with attention to:

- Organic growth
- Acquisition-driven growth
- Divestitures
- Ownership changes
- Consolidation effects

### 8.4 Private-company disclosure gaps / 非上場企業の開示不足

Some companies disclose limited financial information.

In these cases, the dashboard may use estimates, proxy indicators, or partial disclosures.

### 8.5 Business model classification / 事業分類の違い

Business model categories are analytical constructs. They may not match each company’s official segment definitions.

For example, a company may combine production, distribution, licensing, and format revenue in a single reporting line.

## 9. Analytical Focus / 分析上の焦点

The dashboard is designed to support analysis of the following themes.

- Consolidation of global television production groups
- Rise of Super-Indies and mega-studio platforms
- M&A-driven growth in the content production market
- Relationship between scale and profitability
- Shift from production-only models to IP, format, distribution, and licensing models
- Strategic positioning of Banijay, Fremantle, ITV Studios, BBC Studios, Mediawan, and All3Media
- Implications for broadcasters, streamers, production companies, and media technology providers

## 10. Update Policy / 更新方針

The dashboard should be updated when new public data becomes available.

Recommended update triggers:

- Annual reports
- Investor presentations
- Earnings releases
- M&A announcements
- Ownership changes
- New segment disclosures
- Major industry reports
- Significant corporate restructuring

Each update should be recorded in `CHANGELOG.md`.
